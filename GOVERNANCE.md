# Governance

## Overview

Alpha Stochastic Research (ASR) is an independent quantitative finance research laboratory dedicated to rigorous, transparent, and reproducible research.

This governance document defines how ASR projects are managed, how decisions are made, and what standards guide contributors, maintainers, and collaborators.

ASR is committed to building a serious open-source research ecosystem in quantitative finance, financial mathematics, stochastic modelling, scientific computing, and artificial intelligence.

---

## Mission

The mission of Alpha Stochastic Research is to advance open, reproducible, and scientifically rigorous research at the intersection of:

- Quantitative finance
- Mathematical finance
- Stochastic processes
- Risk analysis
- Portfolio optimization
- Financial econometrics
- Scientific computing
- Artificial intelligence in finance
- Open science and education

ASR aims to produce research outputs that are useful, transparent, well-documented, and reproducible.

---

## Founding Role

Alpha Stochastic Research was founded to provide an independent research platform for quantitative finance and scientific computing.

The founder is responsible for:

- Defining the strategic direction of ASR
- Maintaining the scientific identity of the organization
- Approving major research initiatives
- Managing official repositories
- Reviewing institutional documentation
- Protecting the quality and credibility of ASR outputs
- Ensuring alignment with ASR’s mission and standards

The founder has final decision authority on matters related to ASR’s mission, public identity, repository structure, official communications, and long-term research direction.

---

## Maintainers

Maintainers are responsible for the technical and scientific quality of ASR repositories.

Maintainer responsibilities may include:

- Reviewing issues and pull requests
- Ensuring code quality
- Checking mathematical and numerical correctness
- Improving documentation
- Managing GitHub Actions workflows
- Enforcing contribution guidelines
- Supporting reproducibility standards
- Maintaining repository structure
- Helping contributors understand project expectations

Maintainers should act with professionalism, transparency, and respect.

---

## Contributors

Contributors may participate by:

- Reporting bugs
- Suggesting features
- Proposing research directions
- Improving documentation
- Adding references
- Implementing models
- Reproducing papers
- Improving numerical experiments
- Reviewing pull requests
- Participating in discussions

Contributors are expected to follow:

- `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`
- `CONTRIBUTING.md`
- Repository-specific guidelines
- Scientific and reproducibility standards

---

## Decision-Making Principles

ASR decisions are guided by the following principles:

1. **Scientific rigor**  
   Contributions should be mathematically, statistically, or computationally sound.

2. **Reproducibility**  
   Research outputs should be reproducible whenever possible.

3. **Transparency**  
   Assumptions, limitations, dependencies, and data sources should be clearly documented.

4. **Educational value**  
   Projects should help readers understand methods, models, and results.

5. **Open science**  
   ASR supports accessible, transparent, and reusable research outputs.

6. **Long-term maintainability**  
   Repositories should remain clear, structured, and useful over time.

7. **Professionalism**  
   Communication should remain respectful, precise, and constructive.

---

## Project Approval

New ASR projects should generally begin with a research proposal.

A proposal may be approved if it:

- Fits the ASR mission
- Has clear scientific or educational value
- Can be made reproducible
- Has a realistic scope
- Includes relevant references
- Identifies expected outputs
- States assumptions and limitations

Examples of acceptable project types include:

- Reproducible implementation of classic finance papers
- Mathematical finance models
- Stochastic process simulations
- Portfolio optimization studies
- Risk analysis tools
- Financial machine learning experiments
- Educational notebooks
- Technical reports
- Open-source research software

---

## Repository Standards

Official ASR repositories should aim to include:

```text
README.md
LICENSE
CITATION.cff
CONTRIBUTING.md
CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md
SECURITY.md
requirements.txt
src/
notebooks/
docs/
figures/
references/
```

The exact structure may vary depending on the repository type.

Each repository should clearly explain:

- Purpose
- Research context
- Installation
- Usage
- Methodology
- References
- Reproducibility instructions
- License
- Citation information

---

## Pull Request Review

Pull requests are reviewed according to:

- Scientific correctness
- Code quality
- Documentation quality
- Reproducibility
- Numerical validation
- Repository consistency
- Clarity of communication
- Alignment with ASR goals

Major research-related pull requests may require additional review before merging.

---

## Research Integrity

ASR expects all research contributions to follow high standards of integrity.

Contributors should:

- Cite sources properly
- Avoid plagiarism
- State assumptions clearly
- Report limitations honestly
- Avoid overstating results
- Distinguish theory from empirical observation
- Document data and methods
- Make code and outputs reproducible when possible

Research claims should be supported by reasoning, references, or reproducible evidence.

---

## Conflict Resolution

Disagreements should be handled professionally and constructively.

When disagreements occur, participants should:

- Focus on the scientific or technical issue
- Provide evidence or references
- Avoid personal criticism
- Seek clarification before escalation
- Respect maintainer decisions

If necessary, the founder or maintainers may make a final decision to preserve project quality and direction.

---

## Code of Conduct Enforcement

All community members are expected to follow the ASR Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may take action when behavior violates community standards, including:

- Clarification
- Warning
- Temporary restriction
- Removal of inappropriate content
- Permanent exclusion from ASR spaces

See `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md` for details.

---

## Changes to Governance

This governance document may evolve as ASR grows.

Changes may be proposed through pull requests or discussions, but major governance updates require approval from the founder or designated maintainers.

---

## Final Note

Alpha Stochastic Research is built on scientific rigor, transparency, reproducibility, and professional collaboration.

This governance framework exists to protect the quality, credibility, and long-term direction of ASR research.
